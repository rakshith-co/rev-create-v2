# Hero Statement

## Blueprint

Promotional hero split roughly 55/45. Top 55%: flat softly-textured pastel field; tiny letterspaced all-caps kicker (brand · category) with a thin rule at top-left, then a three-line left-aligned power statement filling the width — bold display caps / italic serif lowercase / bold caps ending in a hard period — all set in one deep accent color; below it a small pill badge with one line of all-caps promise microcopy plus a hand-drawn doodle mark. Bottom 45%: warm sunlit lifestyle set with hard shadows on a tiled/textured ground; the hero subject stands center-right and breaks the horizon up into the pastel field, flanked by two or three casual props; a circular seal badge and a row of 3–6 tiny labeled benefit icons ride on or beside the hero, with a thin accent strip of micro-facts (specs, quantity, size) anchoring its base.

## Negatives

No discount starbursts or price-slash flashes — the seal stays a quiet circular stamp, never a promo burst; no "limited offer" flags, no percentage callouts, no more than 6 icons, no patterned or gradient background behind the headline, no drop shadows or outlines on type, max two type families, no prop clutter crowding the hero.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the product pack for the tower/facade hero shot standing in a golden-hour landscaped-podium scene breaking into the pastel headline field; the power statement becomes the emotional claim, the pill badge carries the one casual friend-fact (e.g. "3 acres of central greens, one address"), the icon row becomes 3–4 amenity/location icons, and the micro-facts strip holds config + location + starting price with the seal as a RERA/possession stamp.
