# Social Comment

## Blueprint

Flat white canvas split in three stacked zones. Top 20%: two-line bold black lowercase sans-serif headline, left-aligned, with 1-2 small inline emojis punctuating the copy. Middle 30%: a rounded light-grey native-style social comment card — circular avatar left, bold @handle plus grey timestamp, one casual first-person comment line with emojis, and beneath it a heart icon with like count and a speech-bubble icon with reply count. Bottom 50%: the hero product shot(s) sitting directly on the white background in a clean row, no frame, no shadow clutter, bleeding slightly off the bottom edge.

## Negatives

No starbursts, price flashes or discount badges; only ONE comment card (a pile of comments reads spammy); keep the card iOS-native grey, not brand-colored; no emoji overload in the headline for a luxury read (max one); no fake blue-check unless the handle is real; background stays pure flat white — no gradients, textures or drop shadows behind the hero image.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the product row for a single golden-hour exterior or balcony-view render bleeding off the bottom, and turn the comment into a believable resident/site-visit line ("visited last weekend — the deck view alone sold us"), with the headline selling the emotion the comment confirms. Keep like/reply counts modest and plausible; it becomes a quiet word-of-mouth trust ad rather than hype.
