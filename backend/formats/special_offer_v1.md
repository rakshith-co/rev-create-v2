# Special Offer

## Blueprint

Warm solid-color canvas, single flat background throughout. Top 30%: three small star glyphs centered, then a massive two-line chunky display-serif headline in a dark warm tone, with a one-line all-caps spec subline underneath separated by dot dividers. Middle 50%: product hero centered and large (bundle/box with items fanned symmetrically left and right), a rotated starburst offer sticker overlapping the hero's top-right edge, and a left rail of 3 small stacked circular benefit badges with icons. Bottom 20%: dark price tag/badge anchored bottom-left with tiny eyebrow label above the big price, a horizontal row of 3-4 icon+caption benefit chips beside it, and a full-width single-line marquee strip along the very bottom edge with star separators.

## Negatives

No starburst stickers, no "special intro price" retail framing, no dot-divider spec sublines shouting multiple claims, no icon-chip clutter row, no marquee strip — each of these reads FMCG shelf-promo, not premium. If adapted, cap it at ONE seal (launch/RERA), ONE price line, max two proof badges; keep the background architectural or deep-toned rather than candy-bright.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the fanned product bundle with a centered tower/facade render, turn the starburst into a restrained circular "Launching Now" or "RERA Approved" seal, the price tag into "₹1.2Cr* onwards", and the benefit badges/chips into location-proof stats (metro minutes, acreage, possession date). Even adapted, the multi-badge promo grammar fights the premium editorial tone — use only when the client explicitly wants a price-forward launch blast.
