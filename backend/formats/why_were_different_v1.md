# Why We're Different

## Blueprint

Vertical 50/50 split canvas with a slim angled seam and a bold hand-lettered VS badge pinned dead-center on the divide. Left half is the foil: desaturated near-white panel, oversized black headline "THEM" in the top 15% with a three-word dismissive strapline under it, a muted generic product photo filling the middle 45%, and a 5-row X-mark checklist of shortcomings stacked in the bottom 30%. Right half mirrors the grid in the brand's saturated palette: "US" headline with an accent doodle/star, upbeat three-word strapline, the hero product styled with props in the middle band, a 5-row check-mark checklist bottom-right, plus a small round mantra badge tucked beside it. Both checklists sit on a shared baseline grid so each row reads as a direct one-to-one rebuttal; type is chunky high-contrast display throughout.

## Negatives

No hand-drawn doodles, stickers, cartoon clouds or marker-scrawl type for premium use — replace with a refined serif/clean grotesk and thin rule dividers. No naming or picturing a real competitor; the "them" side stays an anonymous, desaturated archetype. No starbursts, discount flashes, price-slash badges or emoji checkmarks — use restrained tick/cross glyphs. Keep both sides on one photographic grade family (cool-muted vs warm-golden), not clashing candy colors.

## Real-estate adaptation

Left half becomes a cool, desaturated shot of anonymous cookie-cutter high-rise living ("Any 3BHK"); right half is the project's golden-hour hero render ("Life at [Project]"). The mirrored checklists become spec-vs-feeling rebuttals — shared walls vs 3-side-open homes, borrowed amenities vs 5-acre private greens — with the emotional line as the strapline and facts told casually in the list rows.
