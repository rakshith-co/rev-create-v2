# Product in Action

## Blueprint

1:1 full-bleed sunny lifestyle photograph as the single background scene (street, palms, retro architecture, soft warm light) — no panels, everything lives inside the photo. Left 55%: a person cropped knee-up interacts with a comically oversized hero product tilted ~15°, held chest-height so the product face reads at full size; the human touch is the hook. Top-center: brand logo in a hand-drawn script. Right 40%, upper half: stacked copy block on the open sky — one small script kicker line, then a 2–3 word display headline set huge in the brand accent color with hard period, then a one-line subline inside a thin hand-drawn box; below it a small sticker-style badge rotated ~10°. Bottom-right 30%: three product variants in a neat row resting naturally inside the scene (on a surface, not floating). Tiny star/sticker decals in two opposite corners for scrapbook energy.

## Negatives

No holographic or puffy stickers, no doodle stars, no hand-drawn boxes, no bubblegum pastels, no surreal oversized-prop scaling of the property, no exclamation marks, no more than one script accent, no discount starbursts, no cluttered variant cans — keep the human-in-scene idea but grade it editorial: muted palette, serif kicker, single clean badge.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the oversized product for a resident or couple in the foreground of a golden-hour lifestyle scene with the tower rising behind them; the bottom-right variant row becomes three small unit/amenity vignettes (clubhouse, deck, interior) and the sticker badge becomes a restrained RERA/possession or price-anchor chip. The scrapbook-sticker Gen-Z styling itself must be stripped — it reads D2C, not ₹1Cr+ premium.
