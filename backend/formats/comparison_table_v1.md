# Comparison Table

## Blueprint

Full-canvas two-column versus layout, 50/50 vertical split with a hairline center divider: left column sits on a soft brand-tint panel, right column on flat neutral gray. Top band (~20%) holds brand logo + one-line tagline top-left and the competitor label in small caps with a muted descriptor top-right; below it (~25%) each column shows its product image at matched scale — hero packshot left, deliberately plain generic item right. Middle 45% is 5 horizontally aligned icon+text rows: left rows are line-icon + short bold claim with a lighter supporting line, right rows mirror them with muted icons and flat negative statements in gray. Bottom strip (~8%) runs full width in the brand tint with small logos at both ends and a centered punchline tagline.

## Negatives

No red X marks or attack-ad tone, no named competitors, no starbursts or discount badges, no clip-art icons — use thin single-weight line icons, muted grays for the comparison side, generous row spacing, and keep both columns typographically calm so it reads as an editorial spec sheet, not a knock ad. Never use the word luxury for sub-5Cr inventory.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the packshot-vs-bottle pair with a lifestyle or exterior render on the left vs a muted "typical city apartment / standalone tower" visual on the right, and turn the 5 rows into life-quality contrasts (private greens vs road-facing balconies, gated township vs shared access). Avoid naming any builder — frame the right column as a generic status quo, with the footer punchline carrying the emotional close.
