# Headline

## Blueprint

Square canvas on one flat, saturated brand-color background pulled straight from the product's palette — no gradients, no scene. Top 40% is owned by an oversized stacked lowercase headline: a small hand-script intro line ("it's time to" energy), then a 3-line display headline in a heavy rounded serif filling the full width, all in a single contrast color (white). Bottom 60%: the hero product cutout stands dead-center at roughly 45% canvas width, its top edge tucked just under the headline's last line; a small all-caps supporting copy block (4–5 short staccato lines, tiny type) sits on the left rail at vertical center. Loose ingredient/detail elements scatter around the product's base as a foreground layer, breaking the bottom edge for depth; no price, no CTA, no badge clutter.

## Negatives

No starbursts, discount badges, or price flashes; no gradient or busy photographic background — one flat premium tone only; no candy-bright hues (swap to deep ink, forest, champagne or brand dark); no more than two typefaces; keep the scatter elements architectural/botanical, never confetti; headline stays emotional, never a spec sheet.

## Real-estate adaptation

The product pouch becomes a clean tower/facade cutout (or arched entrance render) centered on a deep flat brand tone; the script+display headline carries the emotional promise ("come home to quiet"), the left-rail caps block becomes 4 short location/spec facts told casually, and the base scatter becomes foliage, birds, or a thin skyline strip grounding the building.
