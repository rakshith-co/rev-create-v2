# Star Review

## Blueprint

Full-bleed background is a monochrome brand-color wall (a repeating grid of branded surfaces carrying tiny playful micro-copy lines) that fills 100% of the frame; the hero subject sits center-right at roughly 55-70% frame height, held up from the bottom edge by a hand entering the frame. Left 40-45% is the message column: an oversized white serif-italic pull-quote in quotation marks (3 lines, top-left third), a small 5-star row directly beneath it, then a white rounded review-card UI mid-left — avatar, reviewer name + "verified" tag, star row, 3-4 lines of review body, a text link, and a "N customers found this helpful" footer line. Small brand wordmark locked to the bottom-right corner. Palette is one deep brand color + white; the review card is the only white surface, so it reads as real product-page UI floating over the scene.

## Negatives

No starbursts, price flashes or discount badges; no clip-art gold stars oversized as decoration; no stacking multiple review cards (one card only); no emoji inside the quote; keep the pull-quote serif-italic and unbolded — never switch it to heavy sans shouting; background stays one deep tone, no gradients-of-many-colors or confetti; the review card must look like genuine platform UI, not a designed sticker.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the hand-held product for a twilight facade or hero interior filling the right half (the branded box-wall becomes a full-bleed architectural texture or deep-tone render), and make the pull-quote a resident's emotional line ("We stopped searching the day we saw this view"). The review card becomes a Google-review-style card from a verified homeowner with 5 stars and a "found this helpful" count — borrowed-trust UI that works unusually well for ₹1Cr+ buyers doing diligence.
