# Stat Surround

## Blueprint

Full-bleed top-down lifestyle flat-lay as the background, softly lit, props pushed to the edges. Top 20%: a two-line headline in chunky friendly type set on torn-white-paper strips — line 1 small, line 2 much bigger with a period for punch. Middle 60%: the hero held up by a hand entering from the bottom, face-on and dominant at center; four stat blocks surround it in two side rails (two stacked left, two stacked right), each stat = a small circular line-icon badge, an oversized bold number (e.g. 87%), and a 2-4 word lowercase caption beneath, with thin hand-drawn arrows curving from each stat in toward the hero. Bottom-left 12%: a five-star row over a short two-line social-proof caption. Whole piece runs on a two-tone brand palette pulled from the hero; numbers in the darkest brand tone, backgrounds stay airy and natural.

## Negatives

No starbursts, no discount badges, no price flashes, no more than 4 stats, no invented percentages — every number must be a real project fact. Replace the playful rounded type and doodle arrows with a refined serif/grotesk pairing and thin editorial rules or minimal line arrows; icons stay thin-line monochrome, never colorful clipart. Keep the flat-lay background muted and architectural, not busy kitchen-table clutter. No hand holding the hero for real estate — the tower stands on its own.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the hand-held product for the hero tower render or facade crop at center; the four surrounding stats become hard project facts — mins to metro/airport, % open space, acres, number of amenities, RERA/possession date — and the star-row footer becomes a trust line ("3,200 families chose X" or the builder's legacy years). The headline stays the small-line/big-line emotional one-two, e.g. "Small address. Big life."
