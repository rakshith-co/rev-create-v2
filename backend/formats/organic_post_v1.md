# Organic Post

## Blueprint

Full-bleed candid photograph, zero graphic overlays — every word lives physically in-scene. Upper-left ~30%: a yellow sticky note stuck to a real surface (bench back, window, wall) carries three short handwritten dash-list benefit lines in casual marker, with a small brand URL at the note's base. The product sits in the lower-right two-thirds, resting naturally on the same real-world surface, shot at eye level with shallow depth of field; the top third is soft environmental bokeh (fence, sky, street). Hard natural sunlight and phone-snapshot realism — it should read as an organic post, not an ad.

## Negatives

No graphic text overlays, no logo lockups, no badges, starbursts, price tags or CTA buttons — copy exists only on the in-scene note. Handwriting stays neat and confident, not scribbled; max three short lines plus URL. No studio backdrop, no obvious retouching or AI-perfect sky; keep candid daylight grain. No discount or urgency language on the note.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the placed product with the property itself as the scene — the sticky note goes on a floor-to-ceiling window over the view, a marble kitchen island, or a site-visit railing, so the "surface" is the interior/exterior hero shot. The three handwritten lines become casual friend-facts (location minutes, open-space percent, possession date) with the project microsite URL as the sign-off; works as a scroll-stopping native teaser rather than a hard lead-gen unit.
