# Scroll Break

## Blueprint

Split the frame into two hard zones. Top 35%: flat white band styled as a truncated social post — 2-3 lines of bold black sans-serif, left-aligned like feed body text, written in first person and cut off mid-sentence with an ellipsis followed by a lighter gray "…more" tag. Bottom 65%: full-bleed candid editorial photograph, handheld eye-level in a real environment with natural depth of field, no text on it. No logo, no CTA, no price, no graphic overlays anywhere — the whole unit must read as a screenshotted native post, not an ad.

## Negatives

No logos or brand lockups, no CTA buttons, no price tags or offer badges, no starbursts, no borders or drop shadows on the text band, no studio-lit or render-perfect photography — the photo must stay candid and slightly imperfect, and no text or watermark may touch the photo zone; anything polished breaks the native-post illusion.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the candid product shot with a resident-POV editorial frame — golden-hour balcony view, lobby, or a lived-in interior shot on what looks like a phone. The truncated post becomes a first-person discovery confession about the location or life upgrade ("We stopped shortlisting 3BHKs the day we saw the view from…"), cut off before the payoff so the curiosity carries the click.
