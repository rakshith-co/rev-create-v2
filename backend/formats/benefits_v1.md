# Benefits

## Blueprint

Square split vertically: left ~55% is a full-bleed cinematic hero scene — the product staged in a stylized environment with soft natural props and one sculptural arch element, no text overlaid. Right ~45% is a clean white panel: top-left a two-line headline in large bold sans, line one in near-black, line two repeated-rhythm in the brand accent color. Below it, a vertical stack of five benefit rows connected by a thin vertical rule — each row is a small solid-color circular icon chip (accent colors alternating) on the left, then a bold short benefit title with a 1-2 line light-gray supporting sentence beneath. Bottom-right of the panel: small brand wordmark with a micro-tagline underneath. Generous even white space between rows; everything left-aligned inside the panel.

## Negatives

No starbursts, price tags, discount badges or countdowns; no icons rendered as clip-art emoji; no more than two accent colors in the chips; headline stays two short lines, never a paragraph; hero side stays text-free; no gradient washes over the white panel; no centered text — the panel is strictly left-aligned editorial.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the product scene with a golden-hour tower or lifestyle hero render on the left; the five benefit rows become the project's proof stack — location minutes-to-landmark, land/open-space ratio, clubhouse or amenity signature, builder track record, and possession/RERA fact — with the developer logo and project tagline bottom-right.
