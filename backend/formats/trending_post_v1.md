# Trending Post

## Blueprint

Full-bleed candid lifestyle photo as the background — a real person mid-scene, absorbed in their phone, shot in a warm real-world environment that fills 100% of the canvas. Over it, a floating social-thread screenshot card (rounded corners, app-native UI: small avatar + handle + timestamp row, then a bold sentence-case post title, then 3-4 short casual body paragraphs, then an engagement footer with upvote/comment/share counts) pinned to the left ~55-60% of width and ~65-70% of height, sitting flat like a real screenshot with a soft drop shadow. Product hero stands large in the bottom-right foreground at ~35% of frame height, slightly overlapping the card's right edge to tie the two layers together. All copy lives inside the thread card in native app typography (system-UI sans, mixed weights); no headline or logo outside the card.

## Negatives

No inflated or absurd engagement numbers, no meme slang or emoji spam in the post body, no typos-as-style, no discount stickers, starbursts or price flashes on the card, no more than one thread card per frame, no brand logo slapped outside the screenshot — authenticity dies the moment it reads as an ad. Keep the background photo candid and unstaged; no studio lighting.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the can for a hero render of the tower/facade in the bottom-right corner and make the background a resident on their balcony or in a warm living room checking their phone; the thread becomes a plausible homeowner post — emotional headline ("Finally stopped regretting every rent transfer") with friend-voice facts in the body (possession date, walk-to-metro minutes, why they shortlisted it). Works as a trust/curiosity play but must read like a genuine resident thread, never a scripted review.
