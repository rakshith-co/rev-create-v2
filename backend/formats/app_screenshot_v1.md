# App Screenshot

## Blueprint

Square sunlit tabletop scene. A gently tilted phone mockup fills the left 55–60% of frame, cropped at the bottom edge, showing a clean native app product-detail screen: brand logo header, product hero card up top, a left rail of three stacked spec chips (tiny label over an oversized bold value — two key metrics plus price), bold product name with a one-line description beneath, a star-rating row, a horizontal strip of reviewer avatars with a "See All" link, and a full-width dark rounded-pill CTA button anchored at the screen's bottom. The real product stands life-size in the right 35–40%, level with the phone and slightly behind it, casting a hard directional sun shadow. Fresh context props peek in from the top-right and bottom-left corners; a tiny legal line sits in the canvas's top-right. Palette is pulled from the product; the UI stays white, rounded-card, iOS-clean.

## Negatives

No starbursts, discount badges, or countdown stickers; no candy-bright cartoon palette or doodle illustration on the product; no fake App Store ratings badges or notification-clutter; UI must stay minimal native-iOS white with generous whitespace — never a busy dashboard; shadows stay natural single-source, no floating props or confetti.

## Real-estate adaptation

The physical can beside the phone becomes the tower or facade render (or a framed site-model), and the app screen becomes a premium listing card: project name as the headline, spec chips read carpet area / possession date / starting price, the avatar strip becomes resident or RM testimonials with a 4.8-star rating, and the pill CTA reads "Book a Private Visit". Swap fruit props for landscaping foliage or a skyline edge to keep the sunlit-lifestyle staging.
