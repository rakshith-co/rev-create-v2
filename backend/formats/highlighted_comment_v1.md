# Highlighted Comment

## Blueprint

Square canvas flooded in one flat deep brand color, product-matched. Top 8%: small centered brand wordmark. Next 20%: two-line centered headline — line one bold white sans, line two oversized white italic serif for editorial contrast. Lower 70%: hero product shot centered and large, orbited by review UI cards — one dominant white rounded "highlighted comment" card on the left third (tiny label pill, avatar, name + verified-buyer tag, 5-star row, 4-5 line review body, "read full review" link, helpful-count footer) and three small floating quote chips staggered down the right edge and bottom-left corner, each just avatar + stars + one short quote. Cards cast soft drop shadows on the flat field; hero slightly overlaps the big card for depth.

## Negatives

No starbursts, no discount badges or price slashes, no emoji-stuffed fake comments, no more than one long card plus three short chips (card clutter kills the premium read), no neon accent colors off the brand field, no ALL-CAPS shouting in quotes, no five-star spam rows repeated on every chip edge-to-edge, no plasticky fake avatars.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the pouch for a hero tower/facade render (or a signature interior) on a deep brand-toned field; the highlighted comment becomes a named resident or site-visit testimonial ("We compared six projects before booking here…"), and the mini chips become short buyer quotes or one quote plus two proof chips ("500m from metro", "92% Phase 1 sold"). Testimonial-as-UI is a strong trust device for ₹1Cr+ decisions as long as names feel real and quotes stay understated.
