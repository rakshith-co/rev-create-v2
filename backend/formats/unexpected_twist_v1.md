# Unexpected Twist

## Blueprint

Full-bleed, low-angle lifestyle action shot fills the entire square — subject mid-motion with the product moment in the lower third, dramatic golden-hour sky and landscape behind. Top-left ~45% width, ~35% height: a white rounded app-style review card with small avatar + username, a deliberately LOW star row (1 star), a green "Verified Buyer" pill, and 4–6 lines of small dark review copy whose "complaint" is secretly rave praise (the twist). Bottom-left ~35% of frame: massive stacked condensed serif headline in white, 3 short lines flush-left, cropped tight to the edge. Bottom-right corner: small thin white brand wordmark; no other UI, no CTA button.

## Negatives

No starbursts, price tags or discount flashes; only ONE review card — never a stack of testimonials; the low-star twist must stay singular and legible, not spammy or emoji-cluttered; no CTA buttons or fake app chrome beyond the single card; headline stays editorial serif/condensed, never bubbly or neon; photo must read cinematic lifestyle, not catalog packshot.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the action shot for a golden-hour lifestyle frame of the project — kids on the lawn, tower or clubhouse behind — and turn the card into a mock resident "1-star" testimonial whose complaint is praise ("Weekend getaways feel pointless now. The club is 40 floors down. Unforgivable."), with headline "THE RESIDENTS ARE IN." Use the ironic-review mechanic sparingly at ₹1Cr+ — it wins on wit and social proof but can undercut trust if the sarcasm isn't instantly readable.
