# Reaction Quote

## Blueprint

Full-bleed moody gradient background — deep navy-black at top melting into a lighter atmospheric teal-sky toward the lower third, with a subtle ground/terrain element anchoring the bottom-right corner. Top-left 55% of the canvas carries a stacked 5–6 line all-caps headline in a heavy grotesk, flush left, near-white, written as a spoken reaction ending in a question — with one or two emotional words flipped to italic for conversational cadence. The single hero subject fills the right 55–60%, tilted 30–40 degrees on a diagonal from center-right toward upper-right, slightly overlapping the headline's right edge, with fine debris/particles trailing off it to imply motion and energy. Bottom-right corner holds a small letterspaced wordmark with a one-line tagline beneath it; nothing else on the canvas — no price, no CTA, no badges.

## Negatives

No starbursts, discount stickers, price tags, badges or CTA buttons; headline stays a single spoken-voice question — no feature bullet lists; italics limited to one or two words, never the whole line; no outlines, drop-shadow type or neon gradients; motion debris stays fine and photographic — never cartoon speed-lines; background remains one clean atmospheric gradient, not a busy lifestyle photo competing with the type.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the floating product for a dramatic low-angle crop of the tower (or a signature amenity like the clubhouse edge) emerging on the same upper-right diagonal from mist or golden-hour haze — drop the debris, keep the atmosphere. The headline becomes a resident-reaction or editorial one-liner about the location/lifestyle advantage phrased as a question ("A forest at your doorstep — should we call it unfair?"), with the project logo and positioning line bottom-right.
