# Whiteboard Explainer

## Blueprint

Full-bleed warm lifestyle photo of a wood-framed chalkboard propped on a tabletop, the board filling ~75% of the frame anchored top-left. All messaging is hand-drawn white chalk on the deep-green board: a "Before → After" arrow header across the top ~15% of the board, then two doodle columns underneath — 2-3 simple line icons per side illustrating the old pain (left) vs the new payoff (right) — with one short handwritten fact line under each column, and a chalk ribbon banner carrying the punchline across the board's lower third. A hand holding the hero product enters from the bottom-right corner at ~25% frame width, overlapping the board's frame edge; a small logo lockup sits vertically along the right/bottom edge. Soft props (fruit, decor) blur in the background; everything on the board stays loose, imperfect, hand-lettered.

## Negatives

No cartoon smiley doodles or childish scrawl — chalk strokes must be fine, architectural, blueprint-style line work; max 3 icons per column, no clutter filling every inch of the board; no price-slash math, discount arithmetic or starburst stickers on the board; no neon chalk colors — single white/off-white chalk only; background props stay minimal and defocused, never competing with the board.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the chalkboard for an architect's blueprint/site-plan board with fine white line-sketch icons, and the held D2C pack for a brochure, keys or scale model entering the corner. The before/after becomes a life-upgrade comparison — "renting → owning", "90-min commute → 10 min to metro" — with the chalk fact lines turning into possession-date, location or appreciation stats; works only if the hand-drawn style is kept restrained and draughtsman-like, since cartoon chalk reads too gimmicky for ₹1Cr+ buyers.
