# Behind the Product

## Blueprint

Full-bleed environmental hero photo fills ~85% of the square: the product stands centered at roughly 50% of canvas height, physically planted in its real source setting — raw ingredients/landscape piling around its base in the lower third, soft-focus depth and sky behind. Top-left corner carries the logo with a tiny lowercase tagline beneath; top-right holds a thin-ruled monospace spec card of ~5 all-caps KEY: VALUE rows on small translucent dark tags (like a manufacturing label); a rotated 90-degree microcopy caption runs up the left edge, and a tiny centered copyright line sits just above the footer. Bottom ~15% is a clean light strip with three benefit columns — small line icon, bold short title, lighter one-line subline — separated by hairline vertical rules. Below that, a full-width letterspaced uppercase one-sentence summary runs between two hairlines at the very base.

## Negatives

No starbursts, discount badges, price-slash tags or urgency ribbons; spec card stays data-only monospace (no exclamation copy, no more than 5 rows); footer capped at exactly three icon columns with hairline rules, no filled color chips; background must stay photographic and naturalistic — no illustrated/cartoon environment, no neon gradients, no floating confetti props around the hero.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the product with the tower/facade render standing in the project's real landscaped greens or site context (golden-hour, planting in the foreground); the monospace spec card becomes project facts (CONFIG: 3 & 4 BHK / SIZE: 2,100 SQFT / POSSESSION: 2028 / RERA: no. / TOWERS: 4). The three footer columns become trust stats — acres of open green, clubhouse sqft, builder legacy years — and the base one-liner becomes the location-lifestyle promise.
