# Benefits Checklist

## Blueprint

Single deep saturated brand-color field edge to edge, no gradient. Top 25%: two-line centered headline — line one a short question in cream serif, line two a one-word payoff ("Check.") in an accent-color italic serif. Middle 35%: hero product cutout floating at a three-quarter angle, slightly left of center, lit so one accent detail pops against the dark field, soft contact shadow only. Bottom 40%: a five-row comparison checklist — header row with brand name left and "Other ___" right in small type, then five thin full-width rows separated by hairline dividers, each row carrying a short benefit phrase centered with a filled dot in the left (brand) column and an empty outline circle in the right (competitor) column. All type reversed out in cream/white; accent color used only for the payoff word and product detail.

## Negatives

No starbursts, no discount badges, no red X marks in the competitor column (empty circles only — confident, not combative), no more than five rows, no mixed accent colors, no busy photographic background behind the table, no ALL-CAPS shouting in benefit rows, no price tags or offer stickers.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the product cutout with a golden-hour tower or facade render floating on the dark field; the checklist becomes five buyer-checklist specs (e.g. 3-side open homes, 70% open space, clubhouse size, deck depth, corridor connectivity) with the project ticked against "Most projects in [corridor]" — or drop the competitor column entirely for a single-column "Everything on your list. Check." spec sheet.
