# Key Features

## Blueprint

One flat saturated-pastel field edge-to-edge, no gradients; a large in-context hero shot of the product fills the center ~55-60% of the square canvas, angled slightly right. Top-left stack: small brand logo, then a short 2-3 word headline in bold white caps, left-aligned. Four to six feature callouts orbit the hero on both sides — each a tiny bold-caps label plus a one-line lighter descriptor, tied to its exact spot on the product by a hairline leader line ending in a small dot; a micro tagline sits mid-left, and the right edge carries the tagline repeating as a vertical micro-caps ticker with dot separators. Bottom band (~25%): oversized 2-3 line statement headline in heavy display caps at bottom-left, with a 3-4 line micro body paragraph and a solid dark pill CTA button at bottom-right.

## Negatives

No starbursts, price flashes or discount badges; no more than 6 callouts and no icon chips on them — hairline lines and dots only, never arrows; no boxed/outlined callout cards; single flat background color, no gradients or bokeh; no drop shadows or strokes on type; callout labels stay micro — they must never compete with the two headlines.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the worn-product macro with a hero tower/facade render (or clubhouse/deck hero) and turn the callouts into spec USPs pinned to real spots — deck size, ceiling height, open-space %, clubhouse sqft, minutes-to-metro. Bottom statement headline carries the emotion ("Step light" energy → the lifestyle promise), the body paragraph becomes the friend-voice fact line, ticker repeats the project name or tagline.
