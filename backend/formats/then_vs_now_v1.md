# Then vs Now

## Blueprint

Top 20% band: bold condensed all-caps white headline, two stacked lines, posing a binary choice question. Lower 80%: a single oversized weighing-scale/balance prop centered on a seamless single-color studio backdrop, its two pans staging the comparison — left pan piled with the "old way" cluster (generic clutter, tipped low or high to signal loss), right pan holding the hero product 2-3x in a tight group. Each pan gets a handwritten-script callout with a hand-drawn arrow: negative pain phrase on the left (~upper-left third), positive benefit phrase on the right (~lower-right third). Soft studio shadow under the prop; background stays one flat brand color, no gradients or scenery.

## Negatives

No starbursts, price flashes or discount stickers; no cartoon emoji arrows — callouts must be elegant handwritten script, not marker scribble; keep the backdrop a single muted premium tone (no candy pink for ₹1Cr+); the "old way" pan must read as tasteful metaphor objects, never trashy junk piles; no more than two callouts; no logos floating in negative space.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the literal balance prop for a split-stage comparison: left pan/side carries the old life (cramped high-rise cluster, traffic, grey city miniature), right side carries the project — a miniature tower/villa model or framed hero render elevated higher. Headline stays a choice question ("Which side of the city are you on?"); left callout becomes the pain fact ("90-minute commutes"), right callout the friend-fact benefit ("15 min from the airport, 80% open green").
