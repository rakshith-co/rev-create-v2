# Color Block

## Blueprint

Full-bleed brand-color background in one saturated hue, split into flat tonal blocks of the same color family (lighter panel top, deeper panel bottom-right) so the whole frame reads as a single color field. Top ~45%: two product shots at different scales sit side by side against the tinted backdrop, brand logo large in white above/behind them, with a small badge sticker top-left. Bottom ~55%: a comparison module — left third is a checklist of 3 short benefit lines in bold uppercase inside outlined boxes; right two-thirds is a two-column "US vs THEM" table, US column in the brand color with the product image and check marks, THEM column in muted grey with a bleached generic silhouette and X marks. All type is a single heavy sans in white/brand-dark; icons are simple checks and crosses, no gradients or photography beyond the product cutouts.

## Negatives

No starbursts, no discount stickers, no neon or candy-saturated hues — restrict the color field to a muted premium palette (deep green, ink navy, sandstone). Never name or depict a competitor brand in the THEM column; keep it an abstract grey silhouette. No more than 3 checklist rows, no exclamation marks, no cluttered iconography. Keep the table lines hairline-thin and the type editorial, not infomercial.

## Real-estate adaptation

Replace the product cutouts with a facade render and an interior vignette; the US vs THEM table becomes "This project vs typical projects in the micro-market" (e.g. A-Khata title, 80% open space, 5-min metro vs check/cross rows), and the checklist carries three hard location or spec facts. Works only if the comparison stays abstract — a grey silhouette building, never a named rival — otherwise the format tips D2C-gimmicky for a ₹1Cr+ audience.
