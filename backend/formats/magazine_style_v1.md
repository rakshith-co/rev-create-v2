# Magazine Style

## Blueprint

Full-bleed editorial photo fills the entire 1:1 frame — subject occupies the top 60%, shot low-angle against a monochrome color-washed set, with a small brand logomark pinned top-right. The bottom 40% darkens into a deep gradient panel: a tiny boxed uppercase kicker tag sits left-aligned at the 58% line, then a massive two-line condensed uppercase headline stacks beneath it in heavy magazine-cover type — line one in white, line two switching to the single brand accent color. A small @handle credit anchors the bottom-left corner. Everything left-aligned on one margin; type covers roughly a third of the canvas height.

## Negatives

No starbursts, price tags, discount badges or CTA buttons; no more than one accent color; no centered type or thin airy fonts — the headline must stay heavy, condensed, left-locked; no busy multi-image collage; keep the background a single moody color wash, never a cluttered lifestyle scene fighting the type.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the model-with-product for a dusk facade hero or a resident-on-the-balcony lifestyle shot color-graded to one tone; the two-line headline becomes the emotional claim with the location or possession promise as the accent line (e.g. "NOT JUST AN ADDRESS." / "A WHITEFIELD LANDMARK."), kicker carries the project + configuration tag.
