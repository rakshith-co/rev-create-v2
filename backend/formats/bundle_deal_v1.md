# Bundle Deal

## Blueprint

Solid flat brand-color background, 1:1. Top 20% is a stacked offer header, all centered: a tiny uppercase eyebrow line ("limited time" + event name + date range) in small tracked caps, then the offer headline split across two lines — a small connector word above a very large chunky display number-plus-word ("30% OFF" scale, dark contrasting ink), then a short casual script/handwritten subline naming what the offer covers. Middle 55% is the hero zone: three product units grouped as one trio (center unit forward and largest, flankers angled outward behind), physically tied together with a satin ribbon and bow to sell the "bundle" idea, with small scattered props (ice cubes) grounding the base; soft drop shadows on the flat color. Bottom 18% is a single row of five outlined capsule badges, each a line icon over a two-word benefit label in tiny caps. A one-line fine-print footer (shipping/terms) sits centered at the very bottom edge in micro type.

## Negatives

No starburst or explosion shapes, no strike-through prices, no more than one number on the canvas, no neon discount red/yellow, no ribbon-and-bow kitsch on a property render, no cartoon props or confetti, no more than five badge capsules, no ALL-CAPS shouting in the subline — keep the header ink a single deep tone on one flat premium color.

## Real-estate adaptation

The ribbon-tied can trio becomes three grouped renders (tower elevation flanked by clubhouse and interior, center forward) and the giant %-off headline becomes the one launch number that matters — "Pre-launch from ₹1.4Cr" or "Only 42 homes" — with the date-range eyebrow as the EOI/launch window. The five benefit capsules map cleanly to amenity/location proof points (5 min to metro, 80% open space, clubhouse acreage), but the format stays inherently offer-led, so use it only for genuine launch-window pushes, never evergreen brand work.
