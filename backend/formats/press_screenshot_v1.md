# Press Screenshot

## Blueprint

Square press-clipping on textured cream newsprint, framed by a thin single-accent-color border. Top 8%: newspaper masthead row — tiny all-caps publication label left, issue/date label right, hairline rule beneath. Next 30%: massive black all-caps condensed headline, two stacked lines, left-aligned nearly edge-to-edge, stating a scarcity or demand fact. Middle 35%: single product/hero image centered on the bare paper with a soft cast shadow, its dominant color echoing the frame accent. Bottom 27%: torn-edge paper strip carrying a one-sentence serif editorial lede ("Here's why X is quietly outselling Y this season") with the brand name and 2-3 key phrases in bold, reading like the first line of a news article.

## Negatives

No starbursts, price tags, discount badges or countdown clutter; no glossy 3D bevels or neon gradients; no fake dense newspaper columns or lorem-ipsum filler; keep exactly one accent color (frame + hero echo) — no rainbow palettes; headline stays a factual claim, never "SALE"/"OFFER" shouting; paper texture subtle, not grunge-distressed.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the product cutout for a clean facade/tower render or golden-hour hero shot on the newsprint; the headline becomes the launch-demand fact ("Tower 1 sold out in 9 hours. The waitlist is longer.") and the torn-strip lede explains, friend-casual, why this project is quietly outselling the corridor's marquee names — masthead labels become the city/micro-market and launch-phase issue tag.
