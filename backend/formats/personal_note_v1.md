# Personal Note

## Blueprint

Warm cream single-color canvas. A giant vertical brand wordmark in the accent color runs the full height of the right edge (~15% width), reading bottom-to-top. The left ~70% stacks: a bold two-line accent-color headline top-left, a one-line lowercase kicker ("from the [brand] studio") beneath it, then 12–16 lines of small dark letter-style body copy — one continuous intimate paragraph, ragged-right, tight leading, no bullets. The bottom ~35% holds the product hero photographed large on the same cream ground, cropped by the bottom edge so it feels like it's rising into the note. Two colors total: one accent, one near-black text on cream.

## Negatives

No starbursts, no price tags, no discount stickers, no bullet lists or USP icon chips — the copy must stay one continuous letter, not fragments. No gradients, no drop shadows, no more than two colors plus the cream ground. Never shrink the body copy to make room for badges; whitespace and the long note are the luxury signal.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the product for a twilight facade or clubhouse render cropped at the bottom edge; the manifesto becomes a founder's/architect's note on why the project was built the way it was ("we kept 80% open because..."), with the project name as the vertical wordmark. Pure trust play — ideal for ₹1Cr+ buyers who read before they click.
