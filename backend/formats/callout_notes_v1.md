# Callout Notes

## Blueprint

Full-bleed flat single-color canvas, no gradients. Top 35%: a small handwritten-script kicker line top-left, then a 2-line oversized condensed uppercase headline in ink-black filling the left two-thirds, with a short script accent subline directly beneath it. Middle 50%: the hero subject staged mid-action at center-right (a hand or element entering from the top-right corner adds motion), with 3–4 short handwritten callout notes placed around it — two stacked on the left margin, two on the right — each tied to a specific detail by a thin hand-drawn curved arrow. Bottom 8–10%: a full-width solid black footer bar split into two segments by a thin divider — brand mantra on the left, offer/CTA on the right — set in small caps of the background color.

## Negatives

No starbursts, discount badges, or price roundels; no candy-bright D2C background — use a muted architectural tone (sand, deep green, ivory). Annotations in a refined fine-line italic or elegant hand script, one ink color only, maximum four callouts; arrows must be thin single strokes that never cross each other or the headline. No cartoon doodles, emoji, or marker scrawl; footer strip stays typographic, no percentage-off screamers.

## Real-estate adaptation

Swap the poured product for a golden-hour hero render of the tower or a signature amenity cut out on the flat canvas; the four handwritten callouts become location/spec facts told casually ("5 min to the metro", "3-side-open homes", "80% left as open space", "sunset deck on 32"). The footer offer segment becomes launch pricing or "Book your private walk-through" instead of a discount.
