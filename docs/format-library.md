# Format Library

All 40 Higgsfield Marketing Studio formats, decoded into worded layout blueprints, scored for premium real estate. Machine-readable seed: `backend/seeds/formats.json`.

## Start here — the real-estate shortlist (curated order)

1. **Magazine Style** (9/10) — Swap the model-with-product for a dusk facade hero or a resident-on-the-balcony lifestyle shot color-graded to one tone; the two-line headline becomes the emotional claim with the location or possession promise as the accent line (e.g. "NOT JUST AN ADDRESS." / "A WHITEFIELD LANDMARK."), kicker carries the project + configuration tag.
2. **Lifestyle with Numbers** (9/10) — Swap the D2C product hero for a golden-hour facade, amenity or resident-lifestyle shot; the statistic becomes a trust or scarcity number ("87% of Tower A sold in 45 days", "212 families moved in", "12 mins to the airport") and the subline delivers it casually, like a friend stating a fact.
3. **Scroll Break** (8/10) — Replace the candid product shot with a resident-POV editorial frame — golden-hour balcony view, lobby, or a lived-in interior shot on what looks like a phone. The truncated post becomes a first-person discovery confession about the location or life upgrade ("We stopped shortlisting 3BHKs the day we saw the view from…"), cut off before the payoff so the curiosity carries the click.
4. **Press Screenshot** (9/10) — Swap the product cutout for a clean facade/tower render or golden-hour hero shot on the newsprint; the headline becomes the launch-demand fact ("Tower 1 sold out in 9 hours. The waitlist is longer.") and the torn-strip lede explains, friend-casual, why this project is quietly outselling the corridor's marquee names — masthead labels become the city/micro-market and launch-phase issue tag.
5. **Stat Surround** (8/10) — Swap the hand-held product for the hero tower render or facade crop at center; the four surrounding stats become hard project facts — mins to metro/airport, % open space, acres, number of amenities, RERA/possession date — and the star-row footer becomes a trust line ("3,200 families chose X" or the builder's legacy years). The headline stays the small-line/big-line emotional one-two, e.g. "Small address. Big life."
6. **Key Features** (8/10) — Replace the worn-product macro with a hero tower/facade render (or clubhouse/deck hero) and turn the callouts into spec USPs pinned to real spots — deck size, ceiling height, open-space %, clubhouse sqft, minutes-to-metro. Bottom statement headline carries the emotion ("Step light" energy → the lifestyle promise), the body paragraph becomes the friend-voice fact line, ticker repeats the project name or tagline.
7. **Hero Statement** (8/10) — Swap the product pack for the tower/facade hero shot standing in a golden-hour landscaped-podium scene breaking into the pastel headline field; the power statement becomes the emotional claim, the pill badge carries the one casual friend-fact (e.g. "3 acres of central greens, one address"), the icon row becomes 3–4 amenity/location icons, and the micro-facts strip holds config + location + starting price with the seal as a RERA/possession stamp.
8. **Customer Quote** (8/10) — Replace the product-wearer with a resident or couple shot from behind — on a balcony, lawn or lobby gazing toward the tower/greens — against a deep muted-luxury field (forest green, ink navy, warm sand); the quote becomes one emotional resident line ("the kids just never come inside anymore."), the verification caption becomes "resident since 2025" or "verified homeowner, Tower B", and the stars can stay or give way to a RERA/possession micro-line above the developer logo.
9. **Customer Story** (8/10) — Swap the product flat-lay for a lived-in golden-hour frame of the project — balcony at dusk, clubhouse lawn, or a styled living room — and make the @handle a resident name with the credential line reading "resident since 2025 · Tower A" or "site-visited last month". The handwritten quote becomes one emotional line about living there ("my kids walk to school through a forest now"), stars stay as-is or become a possession/RERA microline; the timestamp keeps the candid-testimonial authenticity.
10. **Star Review** (8/10) — Swap the hand-held product for a twilight facade or hero interior filling the right half (the branded box-wall becomes a full-bleed architectural texture or deep-tone render), and make the pull-quote a resident's emotional line ("We stopped searching the day we saw this view"). The review card becomes a Google-review-style card from a verified homeowner with 5 stars and a "found this helpful" count — borrowed-trust UI that works unusually well for ₹1Cr+ buyers doing diligence.
11. **Media Mentions** (8/10) — Swap the staged product for a moody golden-hour facade or clubhouse still of the project on the lower half, keep the spotlight treatment. Press row becomes real coverage (ET Realty, Times Property, Livemint, local city press) and the pull-quote becomes an actual editor/press line or an awards-jury citation about the project — trust-by-third-party is exactly how ₹1Cr+ buyers de-risk a builder.
12. **Benefits** (8/10) — Replace the product scene with a golden-hour tower or lifestyle hero render on the left; the five benefit rows become the project's proof stack — location minutes-to-landmark, land/open-space ratio, clubhouse or amenity signature, builder track record, and possession/RERA fact — with the developer logo and project tagline bottom-right.
13. **Personal Note** (8/10) — Swap the product for a twilight facade or clubhouse render cropped at the bottom edge; the manifesto becomes a founder's/architect's note on why the project was built the way it was ("we kept 80% open because..."), with the project name as the vertical wordmark. Pure trust play — ideal for ₹1Cr+ buyers who read before they click.
14. **Behind the Product** (8/10) — Replace the product with the tower/facade render standing in the project's real landscaped greens or site context (golden-hour, planting in the foreground); the monospace spec card becomes project facts (CONFIG: 3 & 4 BHK / SIZE: 2,100 SQFT / POSSESSION: 2028 / RERA: no. / TOWERS: 4). The three footer columns become trust stats — acres of open green, clubhouse sqft, builder legacy years — and the base one-liner becomes the location-lifestyle promise.

## Real-estate-native formats to add (not in Higgsfield's 40)

- **Price-Pill Launch** (`price_pill_launch_v1`) — Full-bleed twilight facade or aerial hero, graded to one deep tone; project lockup top-left, one emotional headline mid-left ('The corner of the city that stays quiet.'). Below it, a single quiet rounded pill carries the only number on the canvas — '3 BHK from ₹1.62Cr*' — with an optional second micro-pill for possession year. A compliant dark footer strip runs full-width at the base in micro type: RERA registration number, project/promoter name, disclaimer and site URL — the strip is a fixed system component so every export ships compliant by default. No starbursts, no strike-throughs, one seal maximum.
- **Location Minutes Map** (`connectivity_minutes_map_v1`) — Minimal editorial map: flat deep-tone field with an abstracted road/metro line drawing, project pin glowing at center, 4–5 hairline routes radiating to landmark chips each stating a drive-time fact ('Airport · 22 min', 'ORR · 8 min'), no cartographic clutter. A small hero-render card sits inset in one corner tying pin to product, headline above sells the location emotionally ('Live where the city can reach you — not the reverse.'). RERA microline at the base; every minute figure must be a real, defensible number.
- **Floor-Plan Tease** (`floorplan_teaser_v1`) — A blueprint-toned floor plan fills ~60% of the canvas but is deliberately cropped at one edge; a single zone (the deck, master suite or living span) is tint-highlighted with one hairline callout stating the sqft or dimension fact told casually ('the deck alone is 14 ft deep'). Emotional headline top ('Bigger than your first apartment. Just the balcony.'), CTA line offers the full plan via the lead form — the crop is the lead-gen mechanic. Fine-line draughtsman style only, no cartoon icons, RERA + config microline in the footer.
- **EOI Window (Premium Urgency)** (`eoi_window_v1`) — Flat deep field, centered serif date lockup as the hero of the frame — 'EOI closes 14 July' — with a thin horizontal allocation bar beneath it stating the scarcity as fact, not fear ('42 of 180 homes allocated'). The tower render sits in the lower third, one restrained circular launch seal, developer lockup and RERA strip at the base. Urgency carried entirely by typography and a true number: no countdown clocks, no red, no starbursts.
- **Private Walk-through Invite** (`private_walkthrough_invite_v1`) — Designed as a letterpress event invitation: cream or ink field, thin-rule border, centered serif stack — project name, 'You are invited', experience-centre address and weekend date, RSVP-style CTA ('Book your private walk-through'). One small twilight render or lifestyle vignette sits inside the rule as the only image. Reframes the Meta lead form as an appointment, which is the actual conversion event in RE; RERA microline sits outside the border at the base.
- **Progress Proof** (`construction_progress_v1`) — A 2×2 or split layout pairing dated real site photographs (slab count, tower crane, finished floors — each with a small date stamp) against the final render, under a bold header like 'On schedule. On record.' Footer carries possession date, RERA number and builder-legacy line. This is the trust format RE uniquely needs — de-risking an unfinished product — and no D2C-derived library has it; photos must stay honest and undramatized, no render passed off as site photo.

## How the selector should choose

Route by campaign moment first, tags second: cold-audience/launch moments should pull hook-led and editorial grammars (scroll_break_v1, magazine_style_v1, press_screenshot_v1, hero_statement_v1 — tags: curiosity, launch, scarcity, brand-statement), while consideration and retargeting moments pull proof grammars (star_review_v1, customer_quote_v1, customer_story_v1, media_mentions_v1 — tags: social-proof, trust, testimonial). Rational-buyer or spec-heavy briefs (NRI, end-user upgrader, corridor-comparison) map to stat/spec formats (stat_surround_v1, key_features_v1, benefits_v1, behind_the_product_v1, benefits_checklist_v1), and unknown-builder trust gaps map to personal_note_v1, media_mentions_v1 and the new construction_progress_v1. Within any adset, select one emotion-led, one proof-led and one native/UGC-toned unit — never two formats sharing the same grammar (e.g. two review-card units) — and honor the house copy rule everywhere: headline sells an emotion, subline states the fact like a friend. Scarcity/demand claims (press_screenshot_v1, eoi_window_v1, lifestyle_with_numbers_v1's stat) are only selectable when a real verifiable number exists; unrecognized builders in mystery-mode should avoid press/scarcity name-drops and lean on scroll_break_v1, customer_story_v1 and magazine_style_v1 instead. Every selected format inherits the RERA-compliant footer strip from price_pill_launch_v1 as a system-level component, not a per-format choice.

## Near-duplicate layouts

- keep **stat_surround_v1**, drop stat_surround_2_v1 — Same skeleton: single hero centered with four stat/benefit callouts stacked in two side rails plus a proof footer. stat_surround_v1's number-led blocks (oversized figure + micro caption) are the stronger stat device for RE facts, and its negatives already strip the D2C props; v2 only adds a rotating badge and chip icons on top of the same grid.
- keep **star_review_v1**, drop trusted_review_v1, highlighted_comment_v1 — All three are the identical grammar: oversized pull-quote headline + a single white rounded review-card UI (avatar, verified tag, stars, body, helpful-count footer) floating over a hero on one deep brand-color field. star_review_v1 is the cleanest single-card version; trusted_review only recolors it, and highlighted_comment adds mini quote chips that push it toward card clutter.
- keep **social_proof_v1**, drop social_comment_v1 — Byte-for-byte the same layout: white canvas, bold lowercase headline top, one grey iOS-native comment card middle, hero imagery row bleeding off the bottom. social_proof_v1's three-frame bottom row maps perfectly to the RE exterior/interior/amenity trio, so it's the more adaptable keeper.
- keep **benefits_checklist_v1**, drop why_were_different_v1, color_block_v1, comparison_table_v1 — Four skins of the same brand-vs-generic comparison mechanic (mirrored checklists, US/THEM table, two-column spec rows). benefits_checklist_v1's filled-dot vs empty-circle table on a single deep field is the only version that stays confident rather than combative — no grey competitor photo, no X marks — which is the only register that survives at ₹1Cr+. Also fold then_vs_now_v1's job into this keeper; its balance-scale prop is a distinct visual but the same comparison job done more gimmicky.
- keep **headline_v1**, drop product_spotlight_v1 — Both are flat single-color field, oversized stacked display headline owning the top ~40%, centered hero cutout below with small supporting copy. headline_v1 is the purer grammar (no podium planes, CTA pill or benefit-chip row to strip out) and its script-intro + emotional headline maps directly to the emotion-headline / friend-fact-subline house rule.
